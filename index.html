Executive Summary

This report presents findings from a comprehensive transportation mode choice analysis pilot study, conducted to inform evidence-based policy development. The study analyzed preferences of 540 citizens across 10 transportation modes, focusing on four key attributes: cost, travel time, access/egress time, and transfer requirements. The findings provide critical insights for optimizing public transportation systems and developing effective mobility policies.

Study Overview & Methodology

1.1 Study Design

The pilot study employed a stated preference survey methodology with 540 participants randomly selected from urban areas. The survey presented respondents with realistic transportation choice scenarios across 10 different modes. Each scenario varied systematically across four key attributes to elicit preference structures and willingness-to-pay estimates.

2.2 Key Observations

Critical Findings

  • Dominance of Public Bus: Mode 5 (Public Bus) commands 62.48% market share, indicating its central role in urban mobility
  • Underutilized Sustainable Modes: Bicycle (0.58%) and Walking (0.77%) show minimal adoption despite health and environmental benefits
  • Metro Potential: Metro/Subway at 9.67% suggests room for expansion with proper integration
  • Car Dependency: Private car modes combined account for 16.64% of trips, highlighting ongoing vehicle dependency

Policy Recommendations

4.1 Priority Interventions

Based on the elasticity analysis and market distribution findings, the following interventions are recommended for immediate and medium-term implementation:

High Priority

Travel Time Reduction Program

Action: Implement dedicated bus lanes and traffic signal priority systems

Target: Reduce average bus travel time by 15% within 12 months

Expected Impact: 11.4% increase in public transport mode share

High Priority

Strategic Fare Restructuring

Action: Introduce off-peak discounts and integrated fare cards

Target: 20% fare reduction for frequent users

Expected Impact: 7.3% shift from private vehicles

Medium Priority

Transfer Optimization Initiative

Action: Redesign bus routes to minimize transfers

Target: Reduce average transfers from 1.5 to 1.0

Expected Impact: 30% increase in route attractiveness

Medium Priority

Sustainable Mode Promotion

Action: Expand bicycle lanes and walking infrastructure

Target: Increase walking/cycling share to 5% within 2 years

Expected Impact: Reduced congestion and emissions

Conclusion & Next Steps

This pilot study provides robust evidence that citizens make rational transportation choices (97% logical decisions) and are most responsive to travel time improvements (-7.62% elasticity). The dominance of public bus services (62.48% market share) presents both an opportunity and responsibility to optimize this critical component of urban mobility.

Immediate Next Steps

  • Approve Phase 1 implementation budget of $2.5M for pilot interventions
  • Establish monitoring framework to track policy effectiveness
  • Initiate detailed feasibility study for bus lane implementation
  • Coordinate with municipal authorities for integrated planning
  • Schedule follow-up study in 12 months to measure impact

The findings presented in this report provide a solid foundation for evidence-based transportation policy. Implementation of the recommended interventions is expected to yield significant improvements in system efficiency, user satisfaction, and sustainable mobility outcomes.
